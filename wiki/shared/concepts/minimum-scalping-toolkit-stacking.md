# Profit-taking + stop-loss are the minimum scalping toolkit

A methodological reframe (2026-05-02): the force-close-architecture plan originally required *each*
mechanics change to clear the success bar alone, treating stacked-only GREEN as a degraded outcome. The
reframe says **stacking is the expected ship configuration**, because profit-taking and stop-loss are the
minimum viable toolkit, not alternatives.

## What it is

"A real human scalper always uses both — neither alone is a sustainable strategy." So if Session 01's
£-target ([[first-class-pnl-target-per-open]]) demonstrably moves one behavioural metric (policy-close
fraction, median 0 → 0.255) AND Session 02's stop-close ([[env-stop-close-not-agent-learned]]) moves
another (fc rate / stop-close fraction) AND the stacked cohort clears the bar, the plan ships GREEN
without asterisk — "each tool does one job, you need both together to ship." The scientific discipline is
preserved not by forcing each to clear the macro bar in isolation, but by **per-session behavioural
attribution**: each session's solo cohort documents its own metric shift, so the writeup shows each piece
pulling its weight.

## Why it matters

A correction to over-strict ablation discipline: "one change at a time" is right for *attribution* but
wrong as a *ship gate* when the changes are complementary halves of one capability. Isolate to attribute,
then stack to ship — and let per-component behavioural evidence carry the science instead of demanding each
component independently clear the end-to-end bar. Generalises the single-knob-plan discipline
([[input-pathway-over-head-capacity]]) to the case where the minimum viable change is two knobs.
