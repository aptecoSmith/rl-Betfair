# What “Money Pressure” Means (Betfair Exchange)

## 🧠 The basic idea

**Money pressure** refers to the imbalance of money in the order book that suggests:

> Which direction the price is more likely to move

It’s essentially:

- More money trying to BACK → price likely to **fall**
- More money trying to LAY → price likely to **rise**

---

## 📊 Where it comes from

At any moment, the order book shows:

- Available BACK money (bids)
- Available LAY money (asks)

Example:

| Price | BACK (£) | LAY (£) |
|------|--------|--------|
| 2.00 | 1,200  |        |
| 2.02 |        | 300    |

👉 There is **more BACK money than LAY money**

This suggests:

> 🟢 Downward pressure on price (towards 1.98, 1.96, etc.)

---

## 🎯 Intuition

Think of it like supply and demand:

- BACK money = demand (people want to buy the outcome)
- LAY money = supply (people want to sell the outcome)

---

## 🔥 Simple scenarios

### 🟢 Strong BACK pressure

- Large queues on BACK side  
- Thin LAY side  

👉 Likely outcome:
- LAY side gets eaten  
- Price **moves down**

---

### 🔴 Strong LAY pressure

- Large queues on LAY side  
- Thin BACK side  

👉 Likely outcome:
- BACK side gets eaten  
- Price **moves up**

---

## ⚠️ Important: It’s not just static volume

Naively, you might think:

> “Just compare BACK vs LAY amounts”

❌ That’s not enough

Real money pressure depends on:

- How fast money is being matched  
- Orders being added/cancelled  
- Momentum of traded volume  

---

## 🧠 Better definition

A more accurate view:

> **Money pressure = the dynamic imbalance between liquidity and trading activity on each side of the book**

---

## ⚙️ How traders/bots use it

Bots often derive signals like:

- Order book imbalance (OBI)
- Weighted volume near best prices
- Recent traded volume direction

Then decide:

- Join queue?
- Cancel?
- Cross the spread?

---

## 🔧 Simple proxy (good starting point)

A basic approximation:
pressure = (back_volume - lay_volume) / (back_volume + lay_volume)


Where:
- `back_volume` = sum of top N BACK prices  
- `lay_volume` = sum of top N LAY prices  

👉 Result:
- Positive → BACK pressure  
- Negative → LAY pressure  

---

## 🚨 Why this matters

Money pressure helps answer:

- “Is the queue in front of me likely to clear?”
- “Am I about to get run over?”
- “Should I take the price now?”

---

## 👍 Bottom line

- **Money pressure = which side of the market is stronger**
- It helps predict **short-term price movement**
- It’s a key ingredient in:
  - Entry timing  
  - Exit timing  
  - Queue decisions

# Queue Position in Betfair Exchange

## 🧠 What “queue position” actually means

On the Betfair Exchange, orders are matched using:

> **Price–time priority**

That means:
1. Best price gets matched first  
2. At the same price → **earlier orders get matched first**

---

## 🎯 So “queue position” = your place in line

Imagine this at odds **3.0** on the BACK side:

- £500 already waiting to be matched  
- You place a £100 back bet at 3.0  

👉 You are now:

> **£500 behind in the queue**

---

## 📊 What has to happen for you to get matched?

For your £100 to fill:

- At least **£500 must be matched first** (the people ahead of you)  
- Then your £100 gets matched  

👉 Total traded volume needed at that price = **£600**

---

## ⚠️ Why this matters (a lot)

Most beginners assume:

> “If price touches my level, I get filled”

❌ That’s wrong

In reality:
- Price can sit at 3.0  
- Even trade at 3.0  
- And you still **don’t get matched**

Because:
- There’s still money ahead of you in the queue  

---

## 🔥 Simple example

Order book at 2.0 (LAY side):

- £1,000 available to lay at 2.0  
- You place a BACK bet for £100 at 2.0  

👉 You are behind £1,000  

Now:

- £200 gets matched at 2.0  
- Price moves away  

👉 You get:
- ❌ **0 matched**

Even though:
- Trades happened at your price  

---

## 💡 This is where edge comes from

Good traders care about:

- How big the queue is  
- Whether it’s shrinking or growing  
- Whether they’re likely to get filled  

---

## 🧠 How it shows up in your data

From the stream you’re recording, you can see:

- Available volume at each price  
- Traded volume  

👉 From this, you can estimate:

> “How much volume was ahead of me when I entered?”
And surely we can therefore detect - where am I in the queue and, has the traded volume surpassed where I would have been in the queue - now I'm matched!

---

## ⚙️ How bots use queue position

### Before placing an order:
- Check:
  - Queue size  
  - Recent traded volume  
  - Momentum  

👉 Decide:
- Join queue?  
- Or cross the spread?  

---

### After placing:
- Track:
  - Has volume ahead decreased?  
  - Am I close to getting filled?  

👉 Decide:
- Stay  
- Cancel  
- Move price  

---

## 🚨 Why this breaks most backtests

If you ignore queue position:

- Your bot assumes:
  - Instant fills  
- Reality:
  - You’re stuck in queue  
- Result:
  - Backtest profits vanish live  

---

## 🔧 Rough way to estimate it (using your data)

When you place an order:

1. Record:
   - Available volume at that price  
   - e.g. £500  

2. Assume:
   - That’s your queue ahead  

3. Then track:
   - Traded volume at that price  

4. Fill when:
   - Traded volume ≥ queue ahead  

👉 Not perfect, but surprisingly effective  

---

## 👍 Bottom line

- **Queue position = your place in line at a given price**
- You only get matched after:
  - Everyone ahead of you is matched  
- It’s one of the **main reasons bots fail in live trading**

# What “Crossing the Spread” Means (Betfair Exchange)

## 🧠 The basic idea

The **spread** is the gap between:

- Best BACK price (highest someone is willing to back at)
- Best LAY price (lowest someone is willing to lay at)

Example:

- Best BACK: **2.00**
- Best LAY: **2.02**

👉 The spread = **2.00 → 2.02**

---

## 🎯 “Crossing the spread” = taking the opposite side immediately

Instead of waiting in the queue, you:

- Accept the **current best available price**
- Trade instantly with someone already offering liquidity

---

## 🔥 Example

### 🟢 Not crossing the spread (passive order)

You:
- Place a BACK bet at **2.00**

Result:
- You join the queue  
- You wait to be matched  
- No guarantee of a fill  

---

### 🔴 Crossing the spread (aggressive order)

You:
- Place a BACK bet at **2.02** (the best LAY price)

Result:
- You match **instantly**  
- You skip the queue completely  

---

## ⚖️ Trade-off

### ✅ Pros
- Immediate execution  
- No queue risk  
- Certainty of fill  

### ❌ Cons
- Worse price  
- You “pay the spread”  

---

## 💡 Why it matters for trading bots

Your bot constantly chooses between:

### 1. Join the queue (better price)
- Higher profit potential  
- Risk of not getting matched  

### 2. Cross the spread (worse price)
- Guaranteed execution  
- Lower profit margin  

---

## 🧠 Simple way to think about it

- **Joining the queue** = being a *market maker* (patient)  
- **Crossing the spread** = being a *market taker* (aggressive)  

---

## 🚨 Why this is critical in backtesting

If your backtest assumes:
- You always get filled at your chosen price  

👉 You are effectively assuming:
> You never need to cross the spread

❌ This is unrealistic  

In reality:
- Many profitable trades only happen if you’re willing to:
  - Cross the spread  
  - Or miss the trade entirely  

---

## 👍 Bottom line

- **Crossing the spread = trading immediately at the opposite best price**
- You:
  - Skip the queue  
  - Pay a worse price  
- It’s a core decision in any Betfair trading strategy
