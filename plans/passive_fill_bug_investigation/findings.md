# Passive-fill bug — findings

**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Status:** Root cause identified. Forensic filter buildable on top of existing bet-log data. Fix proposal below.

## Verdict: H2 (matcher fine, fill model wrong)

The matcher in `env/exchange_matcher.py` is correct — its single-price + junk-filter contract is sound and is exercised only on aggressive matches. The bug is in `env/bet_manager.py::PassiveOrderBook.on_tick`.

## What the fill model does (bug mechanism)

For each resting passive order, on each tick:

1. Accumulate **total traded volume on the runner** since placement (`runner.total_matched` delta, line 700-704; same accumulator for both `volume` and `pragmatic` fill_modes).
2. Compute fill threshold = `queue_ahead_at_placement + already_filled_at_this_price_level`.
3. If `traded_volume_since_placement >= threshold` → fill at the order's posted `price`.

Two failure modes combine to produce the phantom fills:

- **Total runner volume is treated as fillable, regardless of price.** Trades happening at LTP 5.10 on Big Dispute (in the 4.5-5.4 band) all contribute to the same `traded_volume_since_placement` accumulator. A passive back resting at **7.40** is filled when total runner volume crosses its queue threshold — even though zero of that volume was traded anywhere near 7.40.
- **`queue_ahead_at_placement` is the OWN-SIDE top-level size at placement** (line 169), not the size at the resting order's price level. For a passive back at 7.40 when the own-side (atb) top was at 4.50 with £10 size, the queue threshold becomes ~£10. The first £10 of runner volume (which on a typical pre-off race takes seconds) triggers the fill at 7.40.

The only safety check is the LTP junk filter on the resting price (line 727-735): if the order's resting price drifts outside `LTP × [0.5, 1.5]`, the tick is skipped (order re-evaluates next tick). For Big Dispute resting at 7.40 with LTP 5.10, the ratio is 1.45 — *just inside* the ±50% band, so the gate let it through.

## Reproducer (Big Dispute pair on Cork EW, 2026-05-10)

| t | LTP | atb top-3 | atl top-3 |
|---|---:|---|---|
| 13:04:10.775 | 5.10 | 4.80/4.70/4.60 | 5.20/5.40/7.20 |
| 13:04:20.908 (open lay@4.70) | 5.10 | 4.50/4.40/4.30 | 4.70/4.80/4.90 |
| 13:04:31.644 (close back@**7.40** *) | 5.10 | 4.30/4.20/4.10 | 4.70/4.80/4.90 |
| 13:04:42.373 | 5.10 | 4.40/4.30/4.20 | 4.90/5.00/5.10 |

\* The 7.40 close-leg price comes from `tick_offset(lay_price=4.70, arb_ticks=N, +1)` in `BetfairEnv._open_paired` (line 3743+), where N is set by the agent's `arb_spread` action (or by `solve_back_price_for_target_pnl` under target-pnl sizing). The 21-tick offset from 4.70 to 7.40 is **a legitimate agent choice**, not a matcher error. The bug is the subsequent fill simulation: the visible lay-side top (atl[0]) never went above 4.90 during the 11-second window the order was open, but the order "filled" at 7.40 anyway because total runner volume exceeded ~£10.

## Rejected hypothesis: H1 (data-file truncation)

The processed parquet captures only top-3 of each side (per `memory/project_book_depth_n3_widen_later.md`), so deeper ladder levels are invisible to inspection. However:

- The close-leg target price (7.40) is generated by `tick_offset` from the aggressive price, **not** by reading the ladder. So data-file top-3 truncation can't have moved the target.
- The fill itself doesn't read the opposite-side ladder at all — it uses only the resting price + total runner volume. So no ladder snapshot is consulted; truncation can't be the source of the fictitious fill.

H1 is ruled out. The fill model is the bug.

## Severity estimate (provisional)

Forensic filter will scan all in-flight reeval bet-logs to quantify. A priori bound on which pairs are vulnerable:

A passive close-leg fill is **suspect** when:

1. The pair has two legs with the same `pair_id`, neither `force_close` nor `stop_close`, and the second leg's price differs from the runner's LTP at fill-time by more than ~20%.
2. The visible opposite-side top-of-book never crossed the resting price during the order's lifetime (placement → fill tick).

Big Dispute satisfies (1) at 45% LTP deviation and (2) fully. Expected hit rate is higher on EW markets (thin books → wider bid-ask, agent posts farther from LTP → more passive fills land where no real trades occurred).

The forensic filter described below will produce per-agent breakdowns.

## Forensic filter design (drop-in, no code change to env)

Apply to existing bet_logs/<run>/<agent>/<date>.parquet files:

For each market_id appearing in a bet log:
1. Load the day's `data/processed/<date>.parquet` and extract `(timestamp, LTP, atb_top_levels, atl_top_levels)` per tick for that market.
2. Group bet rows by `pair_id`. For pairs with exactly 2 legs:
    - Identify the OPEN leg (earlier `tick_timestamp`) and CLOSE leg (later `tick_timestamp`).
    - Determine the OPPOSITE side from the close leg (close-leg is a back ⇒ inspect atl; close-leg is a lay ⇒ inspect atb).
    - For every tick in the data parquet between OPEN time and CLOSE time INCLUSIVE, check whether the opposite-side top reached at least the close-leg price.
        - For a back close at price P: need atl top ≤ P (someone offering to lay at P or lower).
        - For a lay close at price P: need atb top ≥ P (someone offering to back at P or higher).
    - If the opposite-side top **never** crossed during that window → flag as **phantom close**.

For each phantom close:
- Adjusted pair P&L = `open_leg.pnl` only (close didn't actually happen → pair would have ridden naked).
- Phantom P&L = (open_leg.pnl + close_leg.pnl) − open_leg.pnl = close_leg.pnl. Subtract from totals.

Re-aggregate per-agent: `clean_day_pnl = day_pnl − sum(phantom_pnls)`. Also report:
- Count of phantom pairs (out of total pairs).
- Phantom locked_pnl share (£ removed / original locked_pnl).
- Win-market vs EW-market split (test the EW hypothesis directly).

This filter is conservative: it under-counts phantoms when the opposite-side top *did* momentarily cross (which can still be a stale ladder issue, just less obvious) and over-counts when the data file's top-3 truncated a real fill at a deeper level. The 20% LTP-deviation gate excludes the "deep-level real fill" case for the easy population.

## Fix proposal (to implement after operator approval)

**Primary fix — `env/bet_manager.py::PassiveOrderBook.on_tick`:**

Add a gating check before the fill condition (line 745-747):

```python
# A passive order can only fill when the opposite-side top-of-book
# has crossed the resting price during the order's lifetime.
# Track the most extreme opposite-side top observed since placement.
if order.side is BetSide.BACK:
    # Resting back order — needs lay-side to reach the resting price
    # or lower. Track minimum atl top seen since placement.
    if order.opposite_side_extreme is None or snap.lay_top < order.opposite_side_extreme:
        order.opposite_side_extreme = snap.lay_top
    if order.opposite_side_extreme is None or order.opposite_side_extreme > order.price:
        continue  # market never crossed — no fill possible
else:  # LAY
    # Resting lay order — needs back-side to reach the resting price
    # or higher. Track maximum atb top seen since placement.
    if order.opposite_side_extreme is None or snap.back_top > order.opposite_side_extreme:
        order.opposite_side_extreme = snap.back_top
    if order.opposite_side_extreme is None or order.opposite_side_extreme < order.price:
        continue
```

Requires adding `opposite_side_extreme: float | None = None` to `PassiveOrder`. Initialised at placement.

Keep the existing volume-based queue-position model **as a SECOND gate** after the crossing gate (queue can still take time to clear after market crosses). The two gates together approximate real Betfair semantics:

1. Market must cross the resting price (necessary condition; this is the bug fix).
2. Queue ahead must be consumed by traded volume (already implemented).

**Secondary fix (optional, follow-on):** Use the `TradedVolumeLadder` field already in the parquets (per `memory/project_traded_volume_ladder_unused.md`) to track per-price traded volume rather than total runner volume. This makes the queue-position model price-aware. Less load-bearing than the crossing gate; do later.

**Regression test:** add `tests/test_passive_order_book.py::test_passive_back_no_fill_when_lay_side_never_crosses` reproducing Big Dispute (lay-side top stays at 4.70-4.90 across 5 ticks, passive back resting at 7.40 must not fill). Run before pushing the fix.

## Knock-on consequences

1. **Held-out reeval numbers will drop.** The forensic filter applied to the in-flight 14-day reevals should give a "clean" baseline — likely 10-40% lower locked_pnl, particularly on EW-heavy days.
2. **Force-close fills are also volume-based** (same `on_tick` path). Force-close attempts at T−N use the `force_close=True` matcher path (relaxed junk filter) — those go through aggressive matching, NOT passive, so they're NOT affected. Only the natural passive-close pathway has this bug.
3. **The EW-filter probe queued separately may now show a smaller effect.** If most of the EW "edge" was phantom-fill-driven, removing EW after the fix gives a smaller delta. Probe still worth running to disentangle.
4. **Retrain WILL be needed** after the fix. Trained policies learned that posting passive closes at any price would fill given enough runner volume; the post-fix simulator will refuse most of those fills, which changes the gradient signal the GA breeds on. The user has already confirmed they want to retrain.
